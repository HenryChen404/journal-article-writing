# Verification and Revision

Use this reference before publication or when reviewing a draft.

## 1. Fact-check ledger

Every consequential claim should have a row.

Recommended columns:

```text
claim_id
article_location
exact_claim
claim_type
source_1
source_2
verification_status
dispute_or_uncertainty
owner
required_action
```

Claim types:

- fact;
- allegation;
- interpretation;
- inference;
- estimate;
- projection;
- quotation;
- scene detail.

Statuses:

- verified;
- partially verified;
- disputed;
- unsupported;
- needs update;
- source declined;
- source unavailable.

## 2. Verification checklist

### Identity

- spelling;
- pronouns;
- title and organization;
- whether role was current at the time described.

### Time and chronology

- exact dates;
- sequence;
- time zones;
- whether later knowledge is being inserted into earlier scenes.

### Numbers

- numerator and denominator;
- units;
- time window;
- inflation or currency basis;
- sample size and methodology;
- whether totals and percentages describe the same population.

### Quotations

- exact wording;
- context;
- speaker;
- whether edits change meaning;
- translation reviewed or clearly labeled.

### Documents and links

- document version;
- page, section, table, or timestamp;
- working URL or archived copy;
- whether a press release is being mistaken for independent verification.

### Current facts

Recheck immediately before finalization:

- office-holders and titles;
- prices and market values;
- laws, regulations, and court status;
- product features;
- schedules;
- statistics that update frequently.

## 3. Fairness test

Fairness is not equal word count. It requires:

- accurate characterization of relevant positions;
- a meaningful chance to respond to consequential criticism;
- disclosure when a source did not respond;
- inclusion of evidence that complicates the preferred angle;
- distinction between intent, effect, and allegation;
- no loaded language unsupported by facts.

Ask:

```text
What would the strongest informed critic say is missing or misleading?
```

Then either address it or explain why it does not change the article.

## 4. Causality test

For every causal sentence, identify:

- proposed cause;
- observed outcome;
- timing;
- mechanism;
- alternative explanation;
- quality of evidence.

Replace unjustified causality with accurate language:

```text
caused → followed / was associated with / contributed to / sources attributed to
```

Do not weaken strong causal evidence merely to sound cautious; match the verb to the evidence.

## 5. Three-pass revision

### Pass 1: substance

- Does the story hypothesis still match the reporting?
- Are central claims verified?
- Is critical counterevidence included?
- Are key affected actors present?
- Is anything important missing?
- Is any scene, quote, or detail unsupported?
- Is the scope honest?

Output: reporting gap list and factual corrections.

### Pass 2: structure and reasoning

- Does the lead connect quickly to the nut graf?
- Is there one controlling line?
- Does each section perform a unique task?
- Is background delayed until needed?
- Are causes and consequences clearly separated?
- Does the article escalate, deepen, or complicate?
- Is the ending earned?

Output: revised outline and move/delete/merge instructions.

### Pass 3: prose and rhythm

- replace abstract nouns with exact nouns and verbs;
- cut repeated setup and throat-clearing;
- shorten weak quotations;
- clarify pronouns and attribution;
- vary sentence and paragraph length;
- remove clichés and empty transitions;
- keep technical terms only when necessary;
- read aloud for rhythm and ambiguity.

Output: line-edited draft.

## 6. Final red-team review

Review the article as:

- a skeptical editor;
- a directly affected person;
- the subject of the strongest criticism;
- a domain expert;
- a reader unfamiliar with the topic.

List the three most damaging plausible objections. Resolve them with reporting, revision, or explicit limitation.

## 7. Publication note

Provide:

```text
Sourcing note:
Unresolved items:
Requests for comment:
Updates checked at:
Known limitations:
```

Do not call a piece fully verified while consequential rows remain unsupported.
